# 🤖 CSCI 2115 - Theory of Computer Science  

## 📌 Course Description  
This course introduces **fundamental concepts in computer science**, focusing on **finite state machines, automata theory, and complexity analysis**.  

📌 **Part 1: Automata Theory & Models of Computation**  
- Introduction to **finite automata, push-down automata, and Turing machines**  
- Applications of automata in **pattern recognition, parsers, and compilers**  
- Emphasis on **finite automata and their properties**  

📌 **Part 2: Complexity Theory & NP-Completeness**  
- Introduction to **asymptotic notation & algorithm complexity**  
- Analysis of **time & space complexity**  
- Introduction to **NP-completeness** and computational hardness  

---

## 📚 Topics Covered  

### 🔄 Finite Automata & State Machines  
- 🎭 **Deterministic & Non-Deterministic Finite Automata (DFA/NFA)**  
- 📜 **Regular Languages & Expressions**  
- 🔄 **Conversions between DFA, NFA, and Regular Expressions**  

### 📚 Push-Down Automata & Context-Free Languages  
- 🔄 **Push-Down Automata (PDA) and their applications**  
- 📖 **Context-Free Grammars (CFGs) & Parsing**  

### 🖥️ Turing Machines & Computability  
- 💾 **Turing Machines & Universal Computation**  
- 🚧 **Decidability & Undecidability**  

### ⏳ Algorithm Complexity & Asymptotic Notation  
- 📏 **Big-O, Big-Theta, and Big-Omega Analysis**  
- 📊 **Time & Space Complexity**  

### 🔥 NP-Completeness & Computational Hardness  
- ❗ **P vs NP Problem**  
- 🔄 **Reducibility & NP-Complete Problems**  

---

## 🎯 Learning Outcomes  
By the end of this course, students will be able to:  

✔️ Construct and analyze **finite automata, push-down automata, and Turing machines**  
✔️ Understand the **relationship between automata and formal languages**  
✔️ Apply **complexity analysis** to evaluate algorithm efficiency  
✔️ Explain the **importance of NP-completeness** in computational theory
